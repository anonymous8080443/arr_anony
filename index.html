πεRL: Improving Flow-Matching Vision-Language-Action Models via Reinforcement Learning in Latent Noise Chunk Space

Anonymous Authors

We introduce πεRL, a plug-in reinforcement learning framework for flow-matching VLA models. Instead of fine-tuning the full vision-language backbone or directly exploring primitive actions, πεRL freezes the pretrained VLA and learns a lightweight policy over latent noise chunks, which are decoded by the frozen flow action expert into executable action chunks.

Overview

πεRL treats the latent noise chunk as the native reinforcement learning interface of flow-matching VLA models. By keeping the pretrained vision-language backbone and action expert frozen, the method preserves the original action prior while enabling online task improvement through a lightweight noise policy.

Figure 1. Overview. Previous VLA adaptation methods often couple environment alignment and task learning through full-policy fine-tuning or direct action-space exploration. πεRL instead performs reinforcement learning in latent noise chunk space, where selected noise chunks are decoded by the frozen flow-matching action expert into temporally coherent action chunks.

Constrained Exploration

Since all behaviors are decoded by the frozen action expert, exploration remains guided by the pretrained VLA action distribution instead of drifting freely in primitive action space.

Temporal Coherence

Each latent noise chunk corresponds to a full action chunk, keeping the optimization interface aligned with the flow-matching generation process.

Chunk-Level Credit

DroQC evaluates complete chunks and propagates sparse rewards over longer horizons, which is especially helpful for long-horizon manipulation tasks.

Method

πεRL consists of a lightweight behavior noise policy, a frozen flow-matching decoder, and DroQC for chunk-level off-policy learning.

Latent Noise Policy

The fixed Gaussian prior is replaced by a trainable policy that samples latent noise chunks conditioned on the robot state. These chunks serve as structured decision variables for online improvement.

Frozen Flow Action Expert

The pretrained VLA backbone and action expert remain frozen. The learned noise chunk is decoded through the original flow-matching dynamics into an executable action chunk.

DroQC

DroQC learns chunk-level Q-values using dropout-regularized critics, enabling sample-efficient off-policy RL while evaluating complete noise chunks rather than isolated primitive actions.

Figure 2. Framework. The frozen VLA encodes language, visual observations, and state. A trainable noise policy generates a latent noise chunk; the frozen action expert decodes it into an action chunk; DroQC updates the noise policy with a target policy, replay buffer, and dropout critic ensemble.

Method in One Paragraph

πεRL adapts pretrained flow-matching VLA models by learning only in the latent noise chunk space. At each chunk-level decision, a lightweight noise policy selects a latent noise chunk, which is then decoded by the frozen action expert into a temporally coherent action chunk. DroQC trains this noise policy with dropout-regularized Q-functions that evaluate complete chunks, allowing sparse rewards to propagate across longer horizons while preserving the action prior of the pretrained model.
